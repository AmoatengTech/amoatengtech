Impact & Company Journey

Incorporated in June 2023 in Kumasi, Ghana, Amoateng Technologies Limited was founded on the ethos of "Keep Moving Forward." Our technical foundation was sharpened through our participation in the Y Combinator Build Sprint 2022, where our CEO honed rapid prototyping and scalable engineering practices.


In May 2026, our flagship e-waste initiative, Trashless Tech, was submitted to the Amazon Sustainability Pilot Program facilitated by MIT Solve. While we did not progress to Phase II of the challenge, the rigorous application process validated our technical model. The program's framework helped us refine our North American expansion strategy and solidify our commitment to ISO and R2 safety certifications.


Today, we continue to build independently. In the first 9 months of our internal pilot of Trashless Tech, we safely processed 30+ PC units, diverted 575 kg of waste from landfills, and recovered valuable materials with zero toxic exposure. This proven track record drives our mission to transform toxic landfill sites into resource recovery hubs globally.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is your North American expansion plan?

We are incorporating as the United States within 4-8 weeks. We plan to launch pilot programs in high-tech hubs like Austin, Seattle, San Francisco or Toronto within 2-3 months of incorporation.

What materials do you process?

We focus on desktop and laptop units, motherboards, and peripheral electronics. We recover precious metals while safely neutralizing hazardous components like batteries and CRTs.

What certifications are you pursuing?

We are pursuing ISO 14001 (Environmental), ISO 45001 (Health & Safety), and R2 (Responsible Recycling) certifications within the next 12 months.

How does your pricing work?

We charge $500-$1000 per ton during the pilot phase. However, recovered metals offset processing costs, making it cheaper than standard hazardous waste disposal.
